Faq about Mardi Gras

When does Mardi Gras start and end?
The carnival season always begins on January 6, which is King's Day. Mardi Gras (Fat Tuesday), on the other hand, is a different day each year.Tuesday The oil changes every year because Easter Sunday is never the same Sunday every year. Oil Tuesday is always the day before Ash Wednesday.Most visitors plan to arrive no later than the Saturday before Mardi Gras and stay until Ash Wednesday.
Is there Mardi Gras "official"?
No! Mardi Gras is a holiday, just like Christmas and Easter. It belongs to everyone. Do not believe the claims of businesses that offer "official" merchandise from Mardi Gras or the "official" Mardi Gras website. The use of the term "official" is just a way for vendors to sell their products or services to those who do not know better. Products or websites are not licensed by the government for Mardi Gras. Each single parade is responsible for all its merchandise. In some parts of the state, official organizations control their Mardi Gras celebration, but this is not true in New Orleans.
What is the weather usually like during Mardi Gras? What should I pack?
The weather during Mardi Gras can vary from very cold to very hot, almost hot. Sometimes this will change from one end to the other during the same day. It is advisable to lay down the clothes as a cold morning can turn into a hot afternoon. Because you will spend long days outside, pack comfortable shoes and clothes that may get a little dirty. Do not forget to check the forecast for rain. If you forecast storms then you will definitely want to bring a raincoat!
Where can I book a room with a balcony to watch the parades pass by?
The best option is to book a package in the French Quarter or along the parade route that offers a terrace, food and a toilet ticket.
How much does it cost to go to Mardi Gras?
It's free! Not only that, but you will come out with bags of Mardi Gras shot beads. That's why we call it "the greatest free show on the planet!"
I only have a limited time. What is the best time to visit during Mardi Gras?
The weekend before the fat Tuesday is the time to visit Mardi Gras in New Orleans. This is the weekend when Bachus and Andimion, the two big parades of the season, roll through the streets of New Orleans. The streets are busy all day on Saturday and Sunday with parade guards. If you can stay longer than a weekend, Monday (Lundy Gra) and Fat Tuesday are also great days to experience Mardi Gras. Orpheus, another big parade, rolls on that Monday night, and the Zulu Lundy Gra festival continues all day. On a fat Tuesday, walking around the French Quarter and seeing all the costumes and masks is a unique and fascinating experience.
What happens if there is bad weather?
The parades roll in light rain. Officials will only reject if the weather forecast is difficult. Local TV and radio news stations (such as WWL Radio) broadcast hourly updates.
Can I bring my children to Mardi Gras?
In every way, yes! Contrary to popular belief, Mardi Gras is a family celebration. Those of us who grew up in New Orleans feel guilty once our kids have grown up and we keep going to every parade because we used to use the “take the kids” patch! Bring large bags (even large garbage bags!) To hold all the things they will grab. Injections often include toys, stuffed animals, beads and more. It is recommended to take your children to parades in suburban areas like Matayar, which is 10 minutes away.
What are the best ways to get around New Orleans after I arrive?
The best transportation methods will be through taxi / car sharing services, tram or rental car, depending on where you are staying. Driving your vehicle and finding parking spaces will be difficult.
Why is Mardi Gras set on different dates each year?
Mardi Gras' date changes each year as it is connected to Easter, which can take place every Sunday from March 23 to April 25. Mardi Gras Day always takes place 47 days before Easter.
